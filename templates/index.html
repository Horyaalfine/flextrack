FAQ

Common questions

What is H&R insurance and why does it matter?

Hire & Reward insurance is required to carry paying customers' goods as a delivery driver. For Amazon Flex drivers it costs approximately £1.49/hour of actual driving time. It's a legitimate business expense on top of your mileage allowance, and FlexLog is one of the only tools that calculates it correctly.

Does FlexLog work if I claim Universal Credit?

Yes — this is one of FlexLog's core features. The UC report uses the correct 45p/mile rate up to 833 miles per assessment period (then 25p/mile), completely separate from the HMRC calculation. You can also adjust your assessment period dates if they don't match the calendar month.

Why is the mileage allowance method used instead of actual fuel costs?

HMRC's approved mileage allowance (55p/mile for 2026/27) is designed to cover all vehicle running costs — fuel, oil, tyres, servicing, insurance, road tax and MOT. When you use this method, you cannot also claim those costs separately. FlexLog's Actual Cash report lets you track real expenses separately so you can see your true cash position.

What is a Paid CX slot?

A Paid CX (Paid Cancellation) is when Amazon cancels your booked slot with sufficient notice and pays you in full, but you don't make the journey. FlexLog logs this as income with zero mileage and zero H&R insurance, which is correct for both HMRC and UC purposes.

Is my data safe?

Yes. Your data is stored securely on a private PostgreSQL database. Passwords are hashed with bcrypt. Payments are handled entirely by Stripe — FlexLog never sees your card details. You can export and delete your data at any time.

Can I cancel anytime?

Yes — cancel anytime from your account settings. No contracts, no cancellation fees. You keep access until the end of your billing period.

Will FlexLog work for other delivery companies?

FlexLog is currently built specifically for Amazon Flex drivers in the UK. Support for DPD, Evri and other couriers is planned for a future update.
